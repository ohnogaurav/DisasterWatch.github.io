How DisasterWatch Works

1. Crowdsourced Reporting

Our platform allows users to report disasters in real-time. Whether it's a flood, fire, or earthquake, users can submit reports with details like location, type of disaster, and severity. This crowdsourced approach helps us gather comprehensive information from various sources quickly.

2. Report Verification

To ensure the accuracy of reported data, we use a combination of AI algorithms and community verification. AI models filter and verify reports to reduce false information. Additionally, multiple user confirmations and cross-referencing with official sources help validate the data before it is published on the platform.

3. Real-Time Map Updates

Once verified, disaster reports are displayed on our live map. Users can view real-time updates, zoom in on specific areas, and see the severity of incidents. This dynamic map provides critical information to help users navigate and respond to ongoing situations.

4. Notifications and Alerts

Stay informed with push notifications about nearby disasters and significant updates. Our alert system ensures you receive timely information directly on your device, keeping you aware of any critical changes or new reports in your area.

5. Community and Safety

By participating in DisasterWatch, you contribute to a safer community. Your reports and updates aid in disaster response efforts, providing valuable information that helps others stay safe. Together, we create a more informed and prepared community during emergencies.
